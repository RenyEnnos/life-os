This protocol prepares the external research required by #104. It does not prove demand, authorize participant contact, or contain participant data.

## Research question and decision

Test H1 from the [product-positioning brief](2026-07-16-product-positioning-brief.md): an individual professional who reaches the end of a disrupted or overloaded week needs a short process to interpret evidence, make trade-offs and choose a few revisable commitments.

After at least five consenting participants have each had the opportunity to complete three weekly cycles, classify H1 as:

- **confirmed** when at least three participants independently complete two or more cycles and describe a concrete decision, trade-off or easier re-entry attributable to the loop rather than researcher prompting;
- **revised** when the repeated problem or useful outcome differs materially from H1, or manual support is necessary for most completed cycles;
- **rejected** when fewer than three participants complete two cycles, no repeated problem/outcome appears, or the existing alternative is consistently sufficient.

Report counts and contrary evidence. Do not convert this small qualitative sample into market size, clinical benefit or willingness-to-pay claims.

## Recruitment boundary

Recruit 5–10 adults who:

- manage their own weekly commitments in an individual professional context;
- report that a recent week was interrupted, overloaded or difficult to re-plan;
- already use any method, including paper, notes, calendar or a task tool;
- can choose freely whether to join and can complete up to three weekly cycles.

Exclude anyone who:

- is under 18;
- reports to, evaluates, depends financially on, or cannot freely decline the researcher;
- is being recruited because of a health condition or is seeking clinical support;
- would need to disclose employer-confidential, client, financial, health or third-party personal data;
- cannot understand the consent statement in the language used.

Recruit from more than one relationship or work context when possible. Record only a participant code, eligibility answers, recruitment channel and consent state. Do not record identity documents, exact employer, diagnosis, income or contact details in the research record.

## Consent script

Read or send this before collecting any research evidence:

> You are invited to test a short weekly planning loop for up to three cycles. Participation is voluntary. We will ask about how you currently review a week, observe whether you complete the loop, and ask what helped or failed. Please do not enter secrets or sensitive information about yourself, your employer, clients or other people. The research record uses a participant code rather than your name. You may skip any question or withdraw at any time without penalty. Withdrawal stops future collection and, if you request it before the anonymized synthesis is completed, your individual notes will be deleted. Your data will not be used for marketing or AI training. Product use, research recording, and any future marketing use require separate choices. Do you understand and freely consent to participate and to the described research notes?

Record `yes`, date, protocol version and whether recording is separately allowed. Silence or product use is not consent. If recording is declined, take minimal notes. Do not retain audio or video by default.

## Data handling

- Assign a random participant code unrelated to email, initials or account ID.
- Keep contact coordination outside the research record and delete it after the final follow-up or withdrawal.
- Store only eligibility, consent evidence, cycle observations, anonymized quotes and the participant's own stated outcome.
- Paraphrase or redact names, organizations, locations, project titles and third-party details immediately.
- Never copy credentials, raw workspace exports, free-form product databases or screen content unrelated to the question.
- Restrict raw notes to the researcher and maintainer. Delete them within 30 days after the #104 synthesis; retain only aggregate counts, anonymized evidence and the decision.
- Fulfil withdrawal by deleting that participant's notes and recording only that a withdrawal occurred. After evidence has been irreversibly aggregated, explain that an individual contribution can no longer be isolated.
- Do not publish quotes, use research for marketing, or send content to an external model without new, specific consent and a reviewed processor decision.

## Problem interview before product exposure

Ask these questions before naming LifeOS or showing the weekly loop. Follow the participant's language; do not suggest an answer.

1. Tell me about the last time a week went off plan.
2. What did you have available when you tried to understand what happened?
3. What did you do next, step by step?
4. How did you decide what to continue, postpone or abandon?
5. Where did that process become difficult or stop?
6. What tool, person or ritual did you use? What was sufficient about it?
7. When did you return to the plan, if at all? What triggered that return?
8. What consequence, if any, did the unresolved week create?

Ask for a recent concrete example, not opinions about a hypothetical product. Do not ask whether the participant “needs,” “likes,” or “would pay for” the proposed solution.

## Three-cycle observation

After the interview, explain the loop neutrally: review evidence and constraints, choose a few commitments, check in during the week, then reflect. Do not promise focus, reduced overload or better decisions.

For each weekly cycle record:

| Field | Allowed evidence |
|---|---|
| Attempt | started, completed, abandoned, or missed |
| Context | participant's non-sensitive description of the trigger |
| Decisions | count and anonymized description of continue/postpone/abandon choices |
| Re-entry | whether and how the participant returned after interruption |
| Manual support | exact researcher action and when it occurred |
| Existing alternative | what else the participant used and whether it was sufficient |
| Outcome | participant's own description; no inferred clinical or productivity score |
| Friction | observed stop plus participant explanation |

Do not coach completion. Allowed support is limited to access help, restating instructions, scheduling a check-in and resolving a product defect. Log each intervention so human facilitation is not attributed to the product.

## Evidence record

Use one row per observation and keep these columns separate:

| Participant | Cycle/date | Fact observed | Anonymized quote | Researcher inference | Manual support | Contrary evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| random code | 1–3 and date | behavior directly seen or reported | optional, minimally necessary wording | interpretation explicitly labelled | none or exact intervention | evidence against H1 |

Facts describe what occurred. Quotes preserve the participant's words after redaction. Inferences may connect observations but cannot be rewritten as facts. Record abandonment and sufficient alternatives with the same care as successful completion.

## Synthesis gate

The #104 synthesis must include recruitment counts, consent/withdrawal counts, cycle completion and abandonment, re-entry, manual-support frequency, repeated evidence for and against H1, and the final `confirmed`, `revised` or `rejected` classification using the declared rule.

Stop and revise this protocol before continuing if participants disclose sensitive data repeatedly, consent is unclear, the participant population changes, or the research question expands. A single comment does not authorize a feature.
